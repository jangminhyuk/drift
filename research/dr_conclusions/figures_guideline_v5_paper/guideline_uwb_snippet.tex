To examine this separation per sequence, we characterize the nominal ESKF by two properties measured once against ground truth. The bias-to-noise ratio splits the position error into a slowly varying offset and residual scatter: above one, the error is a predictable offset --- the structure the adaptive module removes. The DR headroom \(\max(H_w,\,T-1)\) asks whether either nominal covariance is understated. \(H_w = 1 - \mathrm{rms}\!\left(y - h(x^{\mathrm{gt}})\right)/\mathrm{rms}\!\left(h(\hat{x}) - h(x^{\mathrm{gt}})\right)\) is positive when the raw measurements track ground truth more closely than the filter that fuses them: the filter is under-weighting an objectively better sensor, which a larger \(\theta_w\) corrects. The tail ratio \(T\) exceeds one when the largest measurement errors outgrow what the nominal noise model promises --- NLOS spikes that \(\theta_v\) discounts. Innovation statistics cannot replace either property: a filter that has absorbed a bias appears calibrated.

The constellations occupy distinct regimes, and the verdict follows the quadrant in four of the six deployment environments (anchor constellation \(\times\) pilot mode). Constellations~\#1--\#2 show offset-like error with no headroom: the adapter carries the gain. On constellation~\#1, radius selection over~\eqref{eq:radius_grid} collapses DRiFt onto the adapter --- its extra margin does not cover the second module's cost; constellation~\#2 is the first exception, the one interaction the baseline diagnostics cannot anticipate: validation retains a large \(\theta_w\) whose covariance shaping adds a further eight points on top of the adapter. Constellation~\#4 sits in the bias-and-heavy-tails quadrant --- an offset with heavy tails --- and is synergistic (\(+30\%\) adapter alone, \(+38\%\) DRiFt). Constellation~\#3 is heavy-tailed with little bias: the covariance head has nothing to recenter there and over-conservatizes the filter, so DR alone dominates --- the DR-only exception in Table~\ref{tab:uwb_rmse}. On the manually piloted stress flights, whose error is dominated by aggressive maneuvers, the const3 manuals still fall in the heavy-tailed quadrant, while the const4 manuals are the second exception: the DR layer no longer finds anything to correct, the adapter alone keeps a modest \(+8\%\) median gain, and stacking DR on top costs more than it adds.

Where a sequence lands on the map depends only on the platform and environment, since both diagnostics come from the nominal filter alone; which component wins also depends on the trained module (\Cref{subsec:adaptive_module_exp}). For the module used here, the map is a reliable selector: choosing each sequence's variant by its quadrant, with radii fixed per constellation on the validation trials, comes within five percentage points of the best per-sequence choice on 30 of 41 sequences, with a median gap of 1.3 points.
